PARAM VIR CHAKRA

The Param Vir Chakra is India's highest military decoration awarded for acts of extreme bravery and self-sacrifice in the face of the enemy. Here is more information about the Param Vir Chakra:

1. Significance: The Param Vir Chakra translates to the "Wheel of the Ultimate Brave" and is akin to the Medal of Honor in the United States or the Victoria Cross in the United Kingdom.

2. Establishment: The Param Vir Chakra was established on January 26, 1950 by the President of India. It is awarded for displaying exceptional valor in the presence of the enemy.

3. Criteria: The decoration can be awarded to military personnel, regardless of rank, branch, or service, and is given for the most conspicuous acts of bravery or self-sacrifice in the presence of the enemy.

4. Design: The decoration is a circular bronze disc with a State emblem in the center. It is worn on the left chest with a plain purple ribbon.

5. Recipients: As of now, there have been a total of 21 recipients of the Param Vir Chakra. Each recipient has a remarkable story of courage and valor in the face of adversity.

6. Legacy: The Param Vir Chakra recipients are revered in India as national heroes, and their stories are often shared to inspire the younger generation and honor their memory.

7. Recognition: The Param Vir Chakra is a symbol of the highest level of courage and sacrifice in the Indian Armed Forces, and it stands as a testament to the valor and dedication of the recipients.

The Param Vir Chakra is a prestigious and revered award that symbolizes the extraordinary bravery and sacrifice of Indian military personnel in the line of duty.
